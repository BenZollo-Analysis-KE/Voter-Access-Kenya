🧠 The Sociology of the "Queue" in Kibera
The Problem: The "Time Tax"
With 15,400 voters assigned to Olympic Primary School, the "Queue Time" during a general election can exceed 8 hours.
Sociological Observations
Opportunity Cost: Many youth in Kibera work in the informal sector (Daily wage earners). Spending a full day in a queue means losing a day's income. For a struggling youth, the "cost" of voting is too high.
Voter Fatigue: High density leads to frustration and "Voter Apathy." When the queue doesn't move, people go home.
The GIS Solution
Using Spatial Analysis, we can identify smaller community spaces (churches, halls, or open grounds) within 500m of Olympic Primary to "decentralize" the crowd.
